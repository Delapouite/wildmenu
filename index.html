h – hotel

h moves the cursor to the left

H jumps to top

home row

Did you notice the little bumps under your fingers when they rest on the f and j keys? They are tactile hints indicating the natural position of your hands on a keyboard.

The more you stay around this area, the more you optimize your hands moves which results in an increased productivity and less pain to your wrists. There's no need to reach for the mouse at the other side of your desk when quicker strategies let you teleport in the file where you need to work.

m – mike

mark

m ' ` see mark-motions in the user-guide

Marks are handy bookmarks that can be sprinkled over your documents for recurrent access.

Create a mark on the current character with m followed by its name, which is an arbitrary letter of your choice:

  • lowercase letters are marks local to the current buffer.
  • UPPERCASE letters are global ; they are saved between editing sessions.

For example you can add a V mark at the top of your .vimrc to quickly jump to it.

Then, how to jump to a specific mark? Use either ' or ` keys followed by its name.
