Dealing with text encoding is always a lot of fun, isn't it? If you answered a big "NO", the following tip might save your ass one day.

I once stumbled upon a condition almost like this one "hérésie" == "hérésie" and had a very hard time figuring out why this code returned false.

Every time you have to handle words in foreign locales, especially when it comes from another platform, you immediately suspect something might goes wrong. And in this scenario the é was indeed the culprit.

Your eyes can't really help you to unveil the fallacy. You must inspect the ASCII code with powerful tools. Like ga

By using this shortcut for ascii on the é chars on the left hand side nothing weird was printed : <é> 233, Hex 00e9, Octal 351.

However, ga printed me another story for the ones on the right hand side: <e> 101, Hex 65, Octal 145 < > 769, Hex 0301, Octal 1401.

So while the first occurrences where legit é, the other ones where composed chars from a normal e combined with an acute accent!

To dig deeper in the unicode world, have a look at unicode.vim
